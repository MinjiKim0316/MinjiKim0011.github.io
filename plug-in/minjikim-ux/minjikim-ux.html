User Research

Step1: Interview

User1: 20s female (friend)

Q1) How was the corporation’s application when you tried it?
A) It was fine but there were some difficulties to try it.

Q2) What were the pain points?
A) There were two difficulties.
First, there was no button to go back to home when I needed. Second, I wanted to use the app without logged in. But it required me to sign in if I wanted to use the app.

Q3) What about design? How was it?
A) The design was simple and fine.

Q4) Is there anything you would like to excuse?
A) Well~ just only the difficulties I mentioned before and that’s all.



User2: 30s male (family)

Q1) How was the corporation’s application when you tried it?
A) It looked fine but there were some problems for users.

Q2) What were the pain points?
A) First, as a user and as an employee working in a certain company, there were lots of unnecessary sections and contents existed.
Second, there was no home button. There were “Back” and “Close” buttons. But these buttons were not enough and it made me feel uncomfortable.
Last, when I used date section for a trip plan, it was pretty confused and it was difficult to understand why if the corporation made period part on date section because it was not helpful.

Q3) You said, “there were lots of unnecessary sections and contents existed”. What did you mean here?
A) I meant when you introduced or explained about a place, description such as its location (address), numbers of rooms and bathroom, items (stuff) in the place would be enough. However, the app explained too much unnecessary information. I felt it was annoying.

Q4) Okay. Then, what about design? How was it?
A) Overall, it was pretty fine. But, once you open the corporation’s website using your smartphone, you will see “search” section on the top. Since it was located on the top, it was a bit invisible.

Q5) Is there anything you would like to excuse?
A) Well~ I think I mentioned before will be enough.



User3: 40s female (Relatives)

Q1) How was the corporation’s application when you tried it?
A) It was complicated and uncomfortable

Q2) Why? And How?
A) First, there were no home and Wishlist buttons. If there were no Wishlist section, then why the corporation made “like” icons on the pictures for places. I could not understand.
Second, I wanted to compare places I liked. But, how to compare? I hoped the corporation made a section to compare places so that I could choose a better place.
Last, how to choose date? I wanted to select date for a trip. However, it was very complicated. So, I just stopped trying the app here.

Q3) What about design? How was it?
A) Design was good.

Q4) Is there anything you would like to excuse?
A) Yes, I have one. I could not find rank. I mean if the corporation makes a rank section, it would be great. For instance, places for price; from the cheapest to the highest, places for size; from small or single to huge and double.

Step2: Table

What I learned while doing my project…

Doing user research was interesting. Before I did user research, I had a prejudice for perspectives of different generations. Because of their different ages, I expected they would have different issues using the corporation’s website and application. However, the result showed me I was wrong. There were some different issues but most pain points were common. The user research showed me why doing the research was mandatory for UX.
Also, I was sure I could figure every solution out. While doing user research for my project, I realized there were some issues I needed to co-work with partners though. Likewise, while doing my project, I realized collaborating with partners was mandatory since working on doing research, studying users’ needs based on research, designing, and doing programming alone was overwhelming. Though, doing my UX/UI project has advanced my career growth.
